10 Reasons to Visit Palawan

Voted the world's best island multiple times — here's why Palawan deserves every award it has ever won.

1. World's Best Island

Condé Nast Traveler and Travel + Leisure have both named Palawan the world's best island multiple times. The title is well deserved — no other destination combines this level of natural beauty, biodiversity, and authenticity.

2. Two UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The Puerto Princesa Underground River and Tubbataha Reef are both UNESCO World Heritage Sites — placing Palawan in elite global company for natural and ecological significance.

3. World-Class Diving

From WWII wrecks in Coron Bay to the pristine walls and coral gardens of Tubbataha, Palawan offers some of the finest diving experiences on the planet for all skill levels.

4. The Philippines' Longest Beach

Long Beach in San Vicente stretches 14.7 km of unbroken white sand — the longest beach in the Philippines — and remains largely unspoiled, with carefully planned eco-tourism development.

5. Unique Wildlife

The Palawan bearcat, Palawan peacock-pheasant, Philippine mouse-deer, and hundreds of endemic species exist nowhere else on Earth. Palawan is classified as a distinct biogeographic region.

6. Living Indigenous Culture

The Batak, Tagbanua, and Palaw'an peoples maintain ancient traditions, scripts, music, and ecological knowledge — offering visitors a rare window into Philippines pre-colonial heritage.

7. 1,700+ Islands to Explore

With over 1,700 islands and islets, Palawan offers a lifetime of discovery — hidden lagoons, secret beaches, sea caves, and coral atolls most tourists never reach.

8. Adventure for Every Level

From gentle kayaking in sheltered lagoons to serious technical diving, jungle trekking, cliff jumping, and kiteboarding — Palawan has adrenaline and relaxation in equal measure.

9. Rich Historical Heritage

47,000-year-old cave fossils, Spanish colonial forts, WWII wrecks, and living indigenous traditions — Palawan's historical layers are as deep as its underwater world.
