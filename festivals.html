Palawan's Festivals

From colorful street dances to solemn indigenous rituals, Palawan's festivals celebrate its unique heritage.

June

Baragatan Festival

Puerto Princesa, Palawan

The Baragatan Festival is Palawan's grand provincial celebration, held every June in Puerto Princesa City. "Baragatan" means "trade fair" in Cuyonon, reflecting the festival's origins as a gathering of Palawan's diverse communities. The week-long event features street dancing competitions, cultural shows, trade fairs, agricultural exhibits, indigenous arts demonstrations, and beauty pageants. Tribes from across the province converge to share their traditions, music, and crafts. It is the best opportunity to see all of Palawan's cultural diversity in one place.

May

Paduak Festival

El Nido, Palawan

The Paduak Festival in El Nido celebrates the blossoming of the Paduak tree (Pterocarpus indicus), whose golden flowers signal the start of summer and the fishing season. The festival features fluvial parades in El Nido's stunning bay, street dances in traditional attire, music competitions, and environmental programs emphasizing marine conservation. Local fishing communities give thanks for the sea's bounty, and visitors witness one of the Philippines' most visually stunning festivals set against limestone karst cliffs.

August

Bunduky Festival

Coron, Palawan

The Bunduky Festival in Coron celebrates the Tagbanua people's culture and their deep connection with Coron Island, which is an ancestral domain of the Tagbanua. The festival features traditional music using indigenous instruments, weaving demonstrations, rituals honoring the sea and forest spirits, and a showcase of Tagbanua crafts including intricately woven mats and baskets. It is a rare opportunity to witness living indigenous culture in its authentic setting.

November

Pista'y Dagat

San Vicente, Palawan

The Pista'y Dagat ("Feast of the Sea") is celebrated along San Vicente's Long Beach — the Philippines' longest beach — with a week of marine conservation activities, traditional fishing boat races, beach sports, cultural performances, and seafood feasts. The festival promotes eco-tourism and sustainable fishing practices while celebrating the breathtaking coastal environment that makes San Vicente one of Palawan's most promising eco-tourism destinations.

Cultural Heritage

Palawan's living traditions, arts, and indigenous wisdom.

Tagbanua Script

The Tagbanua people preserve one of the oldest writing systems in the Philippines — a syllabic script related to ancient Brahmic alphabets. UNESCO has recognized it as an Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Indigenous Music

The Batak and Palaw'an peoples create music using traditional instruments — the ukulele-like kudyapi, bamboo flutes, and percussive instruments made from forest materials.

Weaving & Crafts

Palawan's indigenous groups are accomplished weavers and carvers. Tagbanua and Palaw'an woven mats, baskets, and beadwork are prized throughout the Philippines for their artistry and cultural significance.
