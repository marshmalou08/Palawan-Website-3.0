Essential Travel Tips

Plan smarter, travel better. Here are the must-know tips for visiting Palawan.

Best Time to Visit

November to May is the dry season — ideal for island hopping, snorkeling, and beach activities. December to February offers the best weather with coolest temperatures. June to October is monsoon season with rough seas; some tours may be cancelled. The Underground River may close during heavy rain.

How to Get There

Fly from Manila to Puerto Princesa (1.5 hrs), El Nido (1.5 hrs), or Coron/Busuanga (50 min). Multiple daily flights on Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and SkyJet. From Puerto Princesa, vans run to El Nido (5–6 hrs) and Port Barton (3–4 hrs). Fast ferries connect El Nido and Coron.

Budget Guide

Budget travellers can manage on ₱1,500–2,500/day in El Nido or Coron (hostel + local food + one tour). Mid-range is ₱3,000–6,000/day. Luxury resorts (El Nido Resorts, MINILOC) start at $300/night. Island-hopping tours cost ₱600–1,500 per person. Always carry cash — ATMs are scarce in remote areas.

What to Pack

Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory — regular sunscreen damages coral), water shoes for rocky lagoon entries, quick-dry clothing, insect repellent (DEET for jungle areas), waterproof dry bags for electronics, a reusable water bottle, and a light rain jacket. Palawan is casual — leave the formal wear at home.

Safety Reminders

Palawan is generally very safe for tourists. Always follow your tour guide's instructions on boats and in caves. Check sea conditions before booking tours — reputable operators cancel if seas are rough. Protect against mosquitoes, especially near mangroves. Drink bottled or filtered water. Store valuables in your accommodation safe.

Eco-Tourism Rules

No single-use plastics in most Palawan destinations. Do not touch or step on coral reefs. Do not feed wildlife. Do not collect shells, sand, or marine animals. Respect indigenous communities and ask permission before photographing people. Adhere to visitor caps at protected sites like Kayangan Lake and the Underground River.

Transportation Options

Getting around the last frontier.

Bangka Boat

The iconic outrigger fishing boats are your main transport between islands. Hire privately or join shared island-hopping tours. Most tours include a boatman, guide, and life jackets.

Tricycle

The lifeblood of Palawan towns — motorized tricycles (motorcycles with a sidecar) are cheap, plentiful, and great for short hops within town. Agree on the fare before departing.

Van / Bus Transfer

Shared vans (El Nido–Puerto Princesa) and buses cover the main inter-city routes. Book through your accommodation or at designated transfer terminals. Travel time: 5–6 hours.

Fast Ferry

Montenegro Lines and other operators run fast ferries between El Nido and Coron (4–5 hours, scenic route). Also available: Coron–Manila ferry routes for longer adventures.
